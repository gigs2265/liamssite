Syllogism for the Immateriality of the Intellect

  1. Formal thought processes can have an exact or unambiguous conceptual content.
  2. Nothing material can have an exact or unambiguous conceptual content.
  3. Therefore, formal thought processes are not material.

Syllogism for God

  1. Is change a real feature of this universe?
  2. Is change the actualization of a potential?
  3. Can no change be actualized unless something already actualizes it?
  4. Such changes observed would constitute a hierarchical causal series.
  5. Such a series cannot regress infinitely due to that being an absurdity.
  6. There must be a primary mover/pure actualizer.
  7. We can draw conclusions about this primary mover.
